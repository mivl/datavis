Global Job Market

Either if you already work in the Information Technology sector or are simply considering it an option, you may want to analyze the possibilities offered within the job market worldwide. IT comprises different fields of work and making the right career decision may not be a trivial task. Ever wondered if you are being paid within your country's average? Or where are the best salaries? Would it be worth moving abroad to pursue a better job opportunity? Select one of the four different careers below to see how salaries vary from country to country.

Software Engineer

To work as a software engineer, a person must normally have a bachelor’s degree in computer engineering, computer science, or a related field. Many companies will prefer candidates for this job who can show practical experience in programming and coding.

Web Developer

Web developers build the backbone of websites. Employers often require candidates to have a bachelor’s degree in computer science, programming, informational technology, or another relevant fields.

Data Analyst

A data analyst uses data to acquire information about specific topics. This usually starts with the survey process, in which data analysts find survey participants and gather the needed information. The data is then interpreted and presented in forms such as charts or reports.

Network Engineer

Network engineers must be knowledgeable of a variety of networking technologies, such as routing, cryptographic tunneling, firewalls, and others. A bachelor's degree in information technology, computer science, or a related field is generally required for this position.
